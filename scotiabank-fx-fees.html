Skip the FX Fees: Two Scotiabank Cards with Limited-Time Welcome Offers

If you've ever come home from a trip abroad and winced at your credit card statement, you already know the sting of foreign transaction fees. That quiet 2.5% charge on every purchase made outside Canada adds up faster than you'd expect — a $3,000 trip can cost you an extra $75 in fees before you've even thought about it.

Two Scotiabank credit cards eliminate that fee entirely. And right now, both of them are running limited-time welcome offers that end July 1, 2026. This post breaks down what each card offers, who it's best suited for, and why the no-FX-fee feature is more valuable than most people realize.

Scotiabank Gold American Express Card — The Everyday Earner That Travels Well

Annual Fee: $120 Welcome Bonus: Up to 45,000 Scene+ Points Offer Ends: July 1, 2026

No Foreign Transaction Fee: The Star of the Show

Most Canadian credit cards charge a 2.5% foreign transaction fee on every purchase made in a foreign currency. The Scotiabank Gold American Express eliminates this completely. Whether you're paying for a hotel in Portugal, dinner in Tokyo, or booking flights on a non-Canadian website, you pay the posted exchange rate and nothing more.

For frequent travellers, this one feature alone can easily justify the annual fee. Even a single international trip per year can make up the difference.

A Limited-Time Welcome Offer Worth Acting On

Source: Scotiabank

The current welcome offer gives you up to 45,000 Scene+ Points — earned in two parts based on spending thresholds in your first year. Scene+ Points can be redeemed for travel, movies, groceries at Sobeys-affiliated stores, and more. At standard redemption rates, 45,000 points represents meaningful real-world value, and this offer is only available until July 1, 2026.

Earn Points While You Prep for Your Trip

The earn structure on this card is genuinely strong for everyday Canadian spending:

  • 6x Scene+ Points per $1 at Sobeys, IGA, Safeway, and other eligible grocery stores
  • 5x Scene+ Points per $1 at restaurants, fast food, and food delivery
  • 5x Scene+ Points per $1 on entertainment (movies, concerts, sporting events)
  • 3x Scene+ Points per $1 on gas, daily transit, and eligible streaming services
  • 1x Scene+ Point per $1 on everything else

If you use this card for groceries and dining, the points accumulate quickly — especially with the boosted grocery rate at Sobeys-banner stores.

Travel Insurance That Actually Matters

The Scotiabank Gold Amex comes with a solid travel insurance package that punches above its $120 annual fee. Coverage includes emergency medical insurance for trips up to 25 days, trip cancellation and interruption coverage, flight delay insurance, and lost or delayed baggage coverage. If something goes wrong on your trip, this card has meaningful protection in place. Always review the policy details to confirm what applies to your specific situation.

Who Is This Card For?

The Scotiabank Gold American Express is a strong fit for Canadians who travel internationally at least once a year and do a significant portion of their grocery shopping at Sobeys, IGA, or Safeway. If you spend heavily on dining and entertainment on top of groceries, the earn rate is hard to beat at this price point.

It's also a good entry point for anyone new to travel credit cards who wants meaningful perks without a premium annual fee.

Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite Card — The Premium Traveller's Best Friend

Annual Fee: $150 Welcome Bonus: Up to 60,000 Scene+ Points Offer Ends: July 1, 2026

No Foreign Transaction Fee — Again, the Foundation

Like the Gold Amex, the Passport Visa Infinite charges zero foreign transaction fees. This is table stakes for both cards and the reason they make this list. If you're travelling abroad with any frequency, you should not be paying a 2.5% surcharge on your purchases — and neither of these cards requires you to.

Generous Welcome Offer

The current welcome offer on the Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite is up to 60,000 Scene+ Points, earned based on spending thresholds in the first year. This is a step up from the Gold Amex bonus and reflects the slightly higher annual fee. The offer runs until July 1, 2026, so if you've been considering this card, now is the time to act.

Scene+ Points are flexible — you can redeem them toward travel, grocery purchases at Sobeys-banner stores, entertainment, and more. 60,000 points is a meaningful stack of value, particularly for those who travel regularly.

6 Free Airport Lounge Visits Per Year

Source: Scotiabank

This is where the Passport Visa Infinite separates itself from most mid-tier travel cards. Cardholders get 6 complimentary airport lounge visits per year through the Visa Airport Companion Program powered by Dragonpass. This includes access to over 1,300 lounges worldwide.

If you travel a few times a year and have experienced a long layover or flight delay in a crowded terminal, you know how much a quiet lounge with food, drinks, and seating actually matters. At $30–$50 per lounge visit retail, those 6 visits alone cover the majority of the annual fee.

Visa Infinite Perks That Elevate Every Trip

As a Visa Infinite card, the Passport comes with a suite of lifestyle and travel benefits beyond just points:

  • Visa Infinite Concierge: 24/7 access to a personal assistant for travel bookings, reservations, and recommendations
  • Visa Infinite Hotel Collection: Preferred rates and benefits at a curated selection of luxury hotels, including room upgrades where available
  • Visa Infinite Dining and Wine Country Program: Access to exclusive culinary experiences and winery events across Canada

These aren't flashy marketing features — they're genuinely useful for cardholders who travel and dine out regularly.

Earning Points on the Road and at Home

The Passport Visa Infinite earns Scene+ Points on everyday spending, with boosted rates at key categories:

  • 3x Scene+ Points per $1 at Sobeys, Safeway, IGA, and affiliated grocery banners
  • 2x Scene+ Points per $1 on groceries, dining, entertainment, and daily transit
  • Up to 4x Scene+ Points per $1 when booking travel through Scene+ Travel
  • 1x Scene+ Point per $1 on all other purchases

The travel booking multiplier is particularly useful for cardholders who book through the Scene+ Travel platform — it stacks your earn rate significantly on flights and hotels.

Travel Insurance for the Trips That Matter

The Passport Visa Infinite comes with a comprehensive travel insurance package, including emergency medical coverage for trips up to 25 days, trip cancellation and interruption insurance, flight delay insurance, and lost or delayed baggage coverage. As with any credit card insurance, review the specific terms before relying on it — but for most leisure travellers, the coverage is solid.

Which Card Is Right for You?

Both cards solve the same core problem — foreign transaction fees — and both are running their best welcome offers right now. The difference comes down to how you travel and what you value most:

  • Choose the Scotiabank Gold Amex ($120/yr) if you want a strong everyday earner with great grocery and dining rates and solid travel insurance at a lower annual fee.
  • Choose the Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite ($150/yr) if you want lounge access, a higher welcome bonus, Visa Infinite benefits, and the boosted earn rate through Scene+ Travel.

Either way, both offers end July 1, 2026. If you've been on the fence, this is the window to act.
